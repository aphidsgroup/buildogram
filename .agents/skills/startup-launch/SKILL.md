## Traffic Engineering for Launch Day (2026 field addendum)

### Plan the day as arithmetic, not vibes

Fix a total-visits target, then reverse-engineer per-channel quotas — each with its own UTM short link and a named owner. Anonymized reference (an AI Agent product, late 2025): a 500K-visit launch day was assembled as ~400K from 40 creator quote-tweets, plus ~20K each from Reddit threads, geo-targeted YouTube ads, and messaging-group distribution. Final tally: ~800 signups at $6.25 per registration. The lesson cuts both ways — traffic is buildable to spec; conversion is the bottleneck you can't buy.

### Sequencing patterns worth stealing

- **Split objectives across two days**: half your amplifiers push the website on day one, the other half push the leaderboard (PH, etc.) on day two. One day, one goal — otherwise the streams dilute each other.
- **Burst window**: get 50–100 creators publishing inside the same 24–48h. Algorithms reward density, not totals.
- **Platform relay** (from the Manus/Devin post-mortems): days 0–3, X/Twitter builds the conversation → days 3–10, YouTube and newsletters consolidate it → day 10+, Reddit/HN carry the long tail. For a big launch, concentrate ~80% of spend on the same day.

### Hacker News: the 30-minute verdict

The front page is decided in the first half hour — roughly 30 points and 10 comments, posted during the US morning window. Flat at minute 40 = done. Bring 2–3 prepared angles (a "Show" post, an open-source-alternative link, a how-it-works writeup) rather than one perfect post, and translate the winner for regional dev forums — one text, two communities.

### Readiness gate

Don't set the date until **at least 10 external developers are satisfied and still actively using the product**. Pre-launch time split: 60% talking to users, 40% preparing the launch — start asset prep once the product hits "60 points," copy first, visuals later.
