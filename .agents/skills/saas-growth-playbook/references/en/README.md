# SaaS Growth Playbook — MRR from $0 to $50K

Revenue is the only metric that matters. This playbook is organized by MRR milestones.

## $0–$1K MRR: First Paying Customers

Validate willingness to pay before building more.

- **Manual sales**: Founders close the first 10 customers personally
- **Pricing signal**: Charge from day one — free plans delay learning
- **Channel**: Direct outreach to people who already expressed the pain
- **Validation gate**: 5+ customers paying without heavy discounts = signal confirmed

## $1K–$10K MRR: Pricing & Retention

Optimize unit economics before scaling acquisition.

- **Pricing experiments**: Test 2–3x price increases; track conversion AND retention impact
- **Early churn audit**: Exit surveys + session recordings for first-30-day churners
- **Onboarding optimization**: Reduce time-to-value; track activation milestones
- **Upgrade triggers**: Identify usage patterns that predict plan upgrades

## $10K–$50K MRR: Scaling Systems

Shift from founder-driven to systems-driven growth.

- **Activation funnels**: Map and optimize the critical path from signup to "aha moment"
- **Expansion revenue**: Usage-based triggers for upsell, add-on features, seat expansion
- **First marketing hire**: Generalist who can run content + paid + lifecycle
- **Channel diversification**: Add 1–2 acquisition channels beyond your initial one

## Retention Levers

The #1 growth lever is keeping existing customers.

- **Cohort analysis**: Weekly retention curves by signup cohort — spot regression early
- **NPS-driven improvements**: Route detractor feedback directly to product roadmap
- **Re-engagement flows**: Automated sequences triggered by declining usage
- **Health scoring**: Composite score predicting churn risk 30 days in advance

## Benchmarks

Stage-appropriate targets so you know if you're on track.

| Metric | $0–$1K | $1K–$10K | $10K–$50K |
|--------|--------|----------|-----------|
| Monthly churn | <15% | <8% | <5% |
| Net revenue retention | — | >100% | >110% |
| CAC payback | <3 mo | <6 mo | <12 mo |
| Activation rate | >20% | >35% | >50% |
