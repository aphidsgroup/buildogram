# KOL Cold Outreach Templates — DM Scripts That Get Replies

Stop getting ignored. These templates are battle-tested across 500+ successful creator partnerships.

## DM Scripts

Platform-specific templates for Twitter, Instagram, and LinkedIn:

- **Twitter DM formula**: Hook (compliment specific content) → Bridge (shared context) → Ask (clear, low-commitment)
- **Instagram DM**: Comment on 3 recent posts first → wait 48hrs → DM with visual asset attached
- **LinkedIn InMail**: Professional tone, mutual connection reference, specific value proposition
- **Key principles**: Personalize the first line, keep under 80 words, one clear CTA
- **Timing**: Send DMs Tuesday–Thursday, 9–11am in their timezone

## Email Sequences

3-touch cold email templates with 40%+ reply rates:

- **Email 1 (Day 0)**: Subject: "[Their recent content] + quick idea" — personalized compliment + value prop + soft ask
- **Email 2 (Day 3)**: "Bumping this — [add new angle or social proof]" — brevity wins
- **Email 3 (Day 7)**: "Last note — [create urgency or offer exclusivity]" — final attempt with clear deadline
- **Subject line formulas**: Use their name, reference their content, avoid spam triggers
- **Technical setup**: Custom domain, SPF/DKIM, warm-up period before volume sends

## Value-First Approach

How to pitch so creators want to respond:

- **Lead with what THEY get**: Exclusive access, audience value, creative freedom, revenue
- **Show you've done homework**: Reference specific videos, posts, or audience demographics
- **Make it easy to say yes**: Pre-built assets, flexible timelines, low effort required
- **Social proof**: Name other creators who've partnered (with permission)
- **Format options**: Give 2–3 collaboration formats and let them choose

## Follow-Up Cadence

When to follow up, how many times, and when to stop:

- **The 3-touch rule**: Never send more than 3 follow-ups per channel
- **Spacing**: Day 0, Day 3, Day 7 — then stop
- **Channel switching**: If email gets no response after 3, try one DM (not both simultaneously)
- **Seasonal awareness**: Avoid holidays, product launch weeks, or known busy periods
- **Graceful exit**: Final message should leave the door open without pressure

## Partnership Structures

Paid vs barter vs affiliate — which to propose when:

- **Paid flat fee**: Best for large creators (>100K); predictable cost; negotiate based on CPM $15–50
- **Product barter**: Best for early stage; send free pro access in exchange for honest review
- **Affiliate/rev-share**: Best for long-term relationships; 15–30% recurring commission
- **Hybrid (flat + affiliate)**: Lower upfront risk; aligned incentives for ongoing promotion
- **Usage-based**: Pay per signup or conversion; best when you have tracking infrastructure
