# AI Product Launch Playbook — Global Day-One Impact

Launch AI products to global attention. Distilled from the Manus, Devin, and AFFiNE breakout launches.

## The Four Forces Behind Viral AI Launches

In causal order — skipping the first breaks the other three:

- **Product depth is the narrative floor**: hype without substance decays in ~72 hours; the story can only stand as tall as the product is deep
- **Reproducibility sets the emotional ceiling**: users could re-run Manus demos themselves, so testimonials read as discovery — one creator's genuine recommendation pulled 2.8M views. Devin's non-reproducible demos drew hedged coverage; the same creator's favor-share managed roughly 20 likes
- **The influencer flywheel**: founders, operators, and investors with real followings are each worth ten commissioned posts — seed genuine surprise, never obligation
- **The platform relay race**: Days 0–3 X/Twitter ignites the topic; Days 3–10 YouTube and Substack deepen it; Day 10+ Reddit and HN keep the long tail alive. Plan the handoffs, not just the spark

## Demo Design Rules

- Design demos for screen-recording and re-creation — reproducibility is what turns viewers into advocates
- The 2.8M-views vs. 20-likes gap is the cost of a demo users cannot verify themselves

## Channel Fit Before Budget

- Sanity ratio: PH exposure→upvote ≈ 1%, while Reddit exposure→GitHub star runs 5–8% — small budgets belong in niche communities and micro-creators, not leaderboards
- Weekly PH badge ≈ 7x the exposure of daily, monthly ≈ 30x — pick the board before picking the date
- **2C products (consumer / education / apps / games)**: Product Hunt's maker audience is mismatched with regular consumers — at most launch once for the badge, then shift to vertical communities (Reddit, Xiaohongshu, Naver cafes), short video (TikTok / YouTube), and vertical KOLs, with a free product as the conversion hook

## Budget-to-Outcome Benchmarks (observed 2025–2026)

| Ticket | Observed ceiling |
|--------|------------------|
| ~$1K | Hundreds of signups — go community-led, skip the big stage |
| $10–20K | Seven-figure impressions, four-figure signups |
| <$20K total | A 10K-star open-source launch with a team of ≤5 |
