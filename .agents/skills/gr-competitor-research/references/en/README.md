# Competitor Research & Growth Flywheel Playbook — Full SOP

Reverse-engineer how a competitor grew: website evolution, social teardown, flywheel scoring, propagation chains. Battle-tested across 150+ AI startups, including the Lovable case study (Nov 2024 launch: 229K impressions, 4.3M video views, 10K+ Discord).

## The 4-Step Research Framework

- **Step 1 — Website teardown**: trace how the site evolved across stages; output the value-proposition evolution and acquisition structure
- **Step 2 — Social channel teardown**: content categories, posting cadence, KOL collaboration patterns per channel
- **Step 3 — Directory sites** (as needed): traffic sources and placement entry points
- **Step 4 — Ad spend** (as needed): channels, creative strategy, traffic mix
- Tools: Wayback Machine (historical snapshots), SimilarWeb (traffic sources), Semrush (SEO and ads)

## Website Evolution — Three Version Checkpoints

Compare V1 (minimum viable site) / Beta (closed testing) / Launch (public release). For each, record: one-line value prop, page length and structure, user cases, Banner/Pricing/CTA framework, SEO changes, community-channel start points, integrations.

## Social Teardown

- Use SimilarWeb first to find the main traffic source, then go deep on that channel — and prioritize competitors who are strong on it
- **Account matrix**: official (cadence, content mix) / founder (differentiation from official) / DevRel (BD and ecosystem) / creators (stage-by-stage KOL collabs)
- **Publishing rhythm**: closed beta → wide release → long-run operations → Launch Week
- **Platform roles**: X/Twitter = ignition (1–3/day), YouTube = activation (1–3/week), LinkedIn = enterprise acquisition (1–2/week), TikTok = new-audience reach

## Growth Flywheel — 6-Stage Scoring

Score each stage: Activation (what drives the aha moment?) → Referral (in-product virality?) → Acquisition (how are prospects drawn in?) → Retention (community belonging?) → Revenue (free-to-paid path?) → Product (does feedback drive iteration?).

| Signal | Strong flywheel | Weak flywheel |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| Retention | High Discord DAU, sustained UGC | Fast churn |
| Word of mouth | Systematic user-story amplification | No amplification loop |
| Monetization | Clear payment triggers | Fuzzy path |
| Durability | Product–community closed loop | Depends on external spend |

## Propagation Chain & KOL Identification

- **4-phase model**: internal ignition (hours after launch, team posts together) → ecosystem catalysis (1–2 days, partner endorsements) → KOL blow-up (2–3 days, top voices define the narrative) → broad penetration (weeks, multilingual users join)
- **Content effectiveness (validated)**: visual demo > real user success story > competitor comparison > quantified data > feature announcement
- **KOL types**: Traffic King (>100K impressions per post), Topic Starter (deep comment threads), Mindset Shifter ("switching to this for good")
- Single-post teardown: Hook (angle) / Nugget (what compels the share) / Feeling (awe, curiosity, FOMO) / Visual (what comprehension cost it removes)

## Self-Check Before You Ship the Report

Three website versions covered; all major social channels torn down; flywheel strength assessed; propagation claims backed by data; reusable tactics extracted with applicability boundaries; risks and early-warning indicators named; 3+ ICP types; pricing upgrade-path analysis; actionable recommendations.
