# Viral Loop Design — Referral, Invite & Share Mechanics

Products don't go viral by accident. This playbook teaches you to engineer virality into the product itself.

## Viral Loop Anatomy

- The mechanics behind the three loop types: referral loops, invite loops, and content loops
- A viral loop is a product feature, not a marketing campaign — it lives inside the user journey

## K-Factor Math

- Calculate your viral coefficient and model growth scenarios from it
- K tells you whether each cohort of users brings in more users than it took to acquire them — measure it before you invest in loop features

## Incentive Design

- What to offer the referrer and what to offer the referred user — and which incentives don't work
- Two-sided incentives need to reward both parties with something they actually value inside the product

## Share Triggers

- Find the moments in the user journey where sharing feels natural, not forced
- A share prompt at the wrong moment is friction; the same prompt at the right moment is a growth engine

## Case Studies

- How Dropbox, Notion, and Loom built their viral engines
- Study the mechanics behind each engine, then adapt the pattern — don't copy the surface features

## B2B vs. 2C Calibration

This playbook defaults to dev tools / open source / B2B SaaS. If your product is 2C (consumer, education, apps, games):

- Swap core metrics to D1/D7/D30 retention, activation rate, and viral coefficient K — not MRR/CAC/LTV
- Swap cold-start channels to vertical communities, short video, and niche KOLs — not Product Hunt / Hacker News / LinkedIn
- Full 2C adaptation guide with per-country channel data lives in `gingiris-seo-geo/references/2c-adaptation.md`
